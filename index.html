Bio-Inspired AI: Learning from Nature's Designs Nature has spent billions of years refining its designs through the process of evolution. These designs often exhibit remarkable efficiency, resilience, and adaptability. By drawing inspiration from nature, researchers are developing bio-inspired AI algorithms that mimic the principles underlying biological systems. For example, neural networks, the cornerstone of modern AI, are inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. However, researchers are now exploring more unconventional sources of inspiration, such as the flocking behavior of birds, the foraging strategies of ants, or the swarming patterns of fish. By emulating these natural phenomena, AI systems can exhibit collective intelligence, self-organization, and robustness in dynamic environments..

Technology is the application of conceptual knowledge for achieving practical goals, especially in a reproducible way.[1] The word technology can also mean the products resulting from such efforts,[2][3] including both tangible tools such as utensils or machines, and intangible ones such as software. The earliest known technology is the stone tool, used during prehistoric times, followed by the control of fire, which contributed to the growth of the human brain and the development of language during the Ice Age.
